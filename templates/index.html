Send from a trusted address

Use a Gmail or Outlook account you have already used for normal email. Google and Microsoft track sender reputation over time — how often you send, whether people reply, and whether messages get marked as spam.

New or barely used accounts rarely have that history. Cold outreach from them is far more likely to land in spam or Promotions. An older address with real conversations and steady use is the safer choice.

Gmail requires 2FA

App passwords only work if 2-Step Verification is on.

  1. Turn on 2FA in Google Account security.
  2. Create one under App Passwords (pick “Mail”).
  3. Paste the 16-character password below.

App passwords work for personal Outlook/Hotmail accounts with two-step verification on.

  1. Turn on 2FA at Microsoft account security.
  2. Create an app password under Advanced security options.
  3. Paste it below — not your regular Outlook password.
